# Set Score Pack Price Top Chase $ EV / Pack Packs to $50+ ETB ROI Best Deal

Methodology

The Pack Value Score is a 0–100 composite heuristic that ranks sets by how efficiently your money converts into high-value pulls. It is not financial advice.

Formula

Score = w₁ × CostEfficiency + w₂ × ChaseMagnitude + w₃ × PullOdds + w₄ × HitDensity

Each component is min-max normalized across all tracked sets to a 0–100 scale, then combined with configurable weights (adjustable via the sliders above).

Components

  • Cost Efficiency — Lower market price per pack scores higher. Uses the minimum of TCGPlayer low price and eBay 30-day median sold price.
  • Chase Magnitude — Higher top chase card value scores higher. Updated with live eBay sold data when available; falls back to manual estimates in sets.json.
  • Pull Odds — The "1 in N" rate for the top chase card. Smaller N (better odds) scores higher. Rates are community estimates from PokeBeach, CardChill, and pack opening studies.
  • Hit Density — Number of tracked chase cards divided by total set size. Sets with more chases relative to their card pool score higher.

Auxiliary Metrics

  • EV / Pack — Expected dollar value per pack opened, computed as Σ(card_value / specific_pull_rate) across all chase cards.
  • Packs to $50+ Card — Estimated packs to pull any chase card worth $50 or more.
  • ETB ROI — (EV of packs in ETB − ETB price) / ETB price × 100%. Negative means you're paying a premium for the ETB packaging/extras.

Data Sources

  • Sealed prices — TCGPlayer (Playwright scraper) + eBay sold listings (30-day median)
  • Chase card values — eBay sold listings for specific cards, falling back to manual estimates
  • Pull rates — Community data from PokeBeach, CardChill, TCGPlayer Authentication Center pack opening studies. These are estimates, not official rates.
  • Set metadata — Manually maintained in data/sets.json with source URLs
Disclaimer: Pull rates are estimates from community pack opening logs, not official Pokémon Company data. Secondary market prices are volatile and change constantly. The Pack Value Score is a heuristic designed to inform, not a guarantee of returns. Do not spend money you cannot afford to lose on trading cards.
