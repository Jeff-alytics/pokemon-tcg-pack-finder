Score Breakdown

Methodology

The Pack Value Score is a 0–100 composite heuristic ranking sets by how efficiently your money converts into high-value pulls.

Score = w₁ × CostEfficiency + w₂ × ChaseMagnitude + w₃ × PullOdds + w₄ × HitDensity

Components

  • Cost Efficiency — Lower market price per pack scores higher. Uses minimum across TCGPlayer, eBay, GameNerdz, Amazon.
  • Chase Magnitude — Higher top chase card value scores higher.
  • Pull Odds — Smaller “1 in N” rate scores higher. Community estimates from PokeBeach, CardChill.
  • Hit Density — More chase cards per total set size scores higher.

Data Sources

  • TCGPlayer — Sealed product market prices
  • eBay — 30-day median sold + active listings
  • Pokemon Center — MSRP + restock status
  • GameNerdz — Below-MSRP + Deal of the Day
  • Amazon — Lowest current offers
  • r/PokemonTCGDeals — Community-sourced deals
Pull rates are community estimates, not official data. Prices are volatile. This is a heuristic, not financial advice.

Product Guide ?

Booster Pack
10 cards. 1 guaranteed rare or better. The base unit.
+ Cheapest entry point (~$4-5 MSRP)
+ Buy exactly how many you want
– Highest per-pack cost
– No extras (sleeves, promos)
Elite Trainer Box (ETB)
9 packs + 65 sleeves + dice + energy cards + storage box.
+ Accessories worth ~$8-10 on their own
+ Best starter product for new collectors
– Premium price (~$45-55, $5-6/pack)
– Worst per-pack value if you don't need accessories
Booster Bundle
6 packs in a branded box. No extras.
+ Usually ~$22-27 ($3.67-4.50/pack)
+ Slightly below retail per-pack
– Only 6 packs — not enough for consistent hits
– Savings over loose packs are small
Booster Box
36 packs (sealed case). The serious collector's buy.
+ Best per-pack price (~$2.50-3.50/pack)
+ Guaranteed hit distribution (~6 ultra rares)
+ Sealed boxes hold resale value
– $90-140+ upfront cost
– Out-of-print sets can be $200+
